Report Format

Reports are usually divided into three parts:

    Preliminary information
  • Title page
  • Executive summary/synopsis
  • Table of contents
  • List of illustrations
  • Acknowledgements.
    Body of the report
  • A statement of aims including a hypothesis.
  • A rationale for your research (that is why you did it).
  • A description of methodology (that is how you did the research).
  • Your results (that is what you found out).
  • Some analysis (that is an interpretation of what the results meant).
  • Conclusion (and recommendations for action).
    Supporting material
  • References
  • Appendices.

What is Report?

Report

  • Presents information (description and explanation)
  • Makes use of numbered headings, and sub headings
  • Makes use of dot point presentation as well as short concise paragraphs
  • Can be scanned quickly for specific information
  • Uses graphics where possible
  • May end with recommendations
  • Research may be primary and secondary

What is Eassy?

Difference of Eassy from Report

  • Presents an explicit argument
  • Does not require headings
  • Written in paragraphs which are linked clearly to each other
  • Needs to be read in detail to gain understanding of argument
  • Usually no use of graphics
  • Rarely has recommendations
  • Research usually secondary/tertiary sources

Business Report

Business reports vary in their subject matter and length, but their structural layout is much the same as other reports.

  1. Preliminaries
    • Title Page
    • Acknowledgments
    • Table of contents
    • List of illustration
    • Executive summary
  2. Body OF Report
    • Introduction
    • Findings
    • Analysis
    • Conclusion
    • Recommendations
  3. Supporting Materials
    • Appendices
    • References
    • Glossary
    • Abbreviations

Research Report

The purpose of a research report is to provide an account of your research in a particular area.

Your report will need to provide your reader with the following core information:

What was the purpose of your research?

What were the results?

What does it mean?

General structure:

  1. Preliminaries
    • Title Page
    • Acknowledgments
    • Table of contents
    • List of illustration
    • Abstract
  2. Body OF Report
    • Introduction
    • Literature review
    • Method
    • Results
    • Discussion
    • Conclusion
  3. Supporting Materials
    • Appendices
    • References
